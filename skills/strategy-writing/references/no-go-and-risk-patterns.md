# No-Go and Risk Patterns

Every strategy file needs real downside logic.

## Good no-go triggers
- proof burden is too high for the route
- buyers are too concentrated or inaccessible
- local protection blocks practical entry
- route economics depend on a weak assumption
- policy ambiguity is too material

## Good “what would change this conclusion” triggers
- eligibility rule changes
- certification access improves
- a new buyer class emerges
- a competing route weakens
- the producer builds missing capability
