# Indonesia — Biofuel Market Reference

**Last updated:** 2026-04-23  
**Primary audience:** Commercial / BD, supply, and strategy teams  
**Decision supported:** How Indonesia affects feedstock availability, competition, and palm-related route economics

## What This File Helps You Decide
Use this file when the team needs to answer:

**How does Indonesia affect our feedstock position, and should we treat it as a market or mainly as a supply-context competitor?**

The answer is: **mainly a supply-context and competitor market**.

## Executive Takeaway
Indonesia matters because it is one of the most important palm-based biofuel jurisdictions in the world.

Its domestic biodiesel policy changes regional feedstock availability and therefore affects Southeast Asia route economics well beyond its own borders.

## Why This Market Matters
Indonesia matters because domestic biodiesel policy can absorb huge amounts of palm-based supply. The current reference point is B40 implementation from 2025, with B50 timing still uncertain.

That affects:
- feedstock availability,
- regional pricing,
- and how much palm-side material is realistically available for export-oriented premium pathways.

## How The Market Actually Works
Indonesia uses strong domestic biodiesel policy to absorb local production and support local energy and palm-sector objectives.

### In plain English
Indonesia is usually more important for what it does to supply conditions than for what it offers as an export destination.

## Market Overview
The country's relevance in this repository is mainly through:
- domestic biodiesel mandate strength,
- palm-oil system influence,
- and the effect of domestic-demand pull on broader Southeast Asia route economics.

## In-Scope Fuel Pathways
- palm-based biodiesel
- selected waste-stream implications such as POME-linked routes

## Commercial Reading
Do not treat Indonesia as a first-wave destination market for the strategy developed in this repository.

Treat it as:
- a supply-shaping market,
- a palm-side competition context,
- and a feedstock-availability driver.

## What This Means For Our Team
- Watch Indonesia to understand palm-side feedstock pressure.
- Use it in feedstock-risk planning.
- Do not confuse domestic biodiesel scale with export-market attractiveness.
