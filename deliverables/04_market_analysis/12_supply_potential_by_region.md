# Supply Potential by Region

**Last updated:** 2026-04-21
**Primary audience:** Commercial / BD team assessing regional supply bases and their relevance to global demand
**Decision supported:** Which regional supply bases can plausibly serve global compliance markets

## Analytical Question
How do regional supply bases compare and which ones are likely relevant to global demand centers?

### How To Read This File
This file should be read as a supply-side counterpart to the global market comparison. The strongest opportunity is not only about demand. It is also about which supply can realistically reach that demand.

## Southeast Asia Supply Deep Dive

**Strongest supply classes:**
- UCO collection and export — established infrastructure, growing volume
- POME from palm oil mills — large waste stream, underutilized for biofuel
- Palm residues (EFB, fronds) — lignocellulosic potential
- Animal fats — slaughterhouse and rendering waste
- Cassava — conventional bioethanol feedstock

**Conversion capacity:**
- Biodiesel: existing capacity in Indonesia, Malaysia, Thailand
- HVO: growing capacity (some plants operational, more planned)
- Bioethanol: existing in Thailand, some in Indonesia
- Biogas: emerging from palm oil mill waste

**Logistics:**
- Established shipping routes to Rotterdam (EU entry point)
- Established trading relationships with European and Asian buyers
- ISCC certification infrastructure exists in the region

## Supply Gap Analysis

| Market | Domestic supply gap | Import need | SE Asia fit |
|---|---|---|---|
| Germany | High (limited domestic waste feedstock) | High | Strong |
| Netherlands | High (trading hub, not producer) | High | Strong |
| France | Medium (domestic rapeseed production) | Medium | Medium |
| US federal | Medium (domestic production large but pathway-specific import windows exist) | Medium | Medium |
| California | Medium (limited in-state production) | Medium | Medium (CI penalty) |
| UK | High (limited domestic production) | High | Medium-High |
| Japan | High (minimal domestic production) | High | Medium (no credits) |

### What this table reveals
The supply gap analysis shows where imports actually matter. The strongest import markets for SE Asia supply are Germany, Netherlands, UK, and France. US federal is less import-hungry because of large domestic production, but even a small share of a large US market can be commercially meaningful.

## Disqualifier Conditions
A supply route should be reconsidered if:
- domestic production in the destination market already saturates demand for the specific pathway
- the supply gap is real but the market does not offer enough premium to justify export logistics
- the destination market's compliance design penalizes long-distance supply (California CI penalty)

## Worked Example: Why Germany's Supply Gap Matters More Than Japan's
**Scenario:** Both Germany and Japan have high import need. A producer can target either.

1. Germany's supply gap is paired with strong compliance demand and tradable credits.
2. Japan's supply gap is paired with soft policy demand and no credits.
3. The import need is similar. The commercial value is not.
4. A supply gap only matters when paired with demand that rewards the supply.

### What this reveals
Import openness alone does not create opportunity. Import openness plus premium demand does.
