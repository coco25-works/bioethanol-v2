# Southeast Asia Supply Base Analysis

**Last updated:** 2026-04-23
**Primary audience:** Commercial / BD team assessing SE Asia supply capacity and feedstock availability
**Decision supported:** What can SE Asia-based producers realistically supply to global compliance markets

## Analytical Question
What is the SE Asia supply base for bio-based fuels and how does it map onto global demand?

### What This File Explains
The strongest export strategy in this repository depends on what SE Asia can actually supply. This file maps the real feedstock and conversion capacity available in the region.

## Supply Base Overview

| Country | Key feedstocks | Existing capacity | Growth potential |
|---|---|---|---|
| Indonesia | Palm oil, POME, UCO | Large biodiesel (B40 mandate) | High (waste-based pathways) |
| Malaysia | Palm oil, POME, UCO | Large biodiesel (B10 baseline; B20 pilots/targeted use; B15/B12 rollout under watch) | High (waste-based pathways) |
| Thailand | Sugarcane, cassava, palm | Ethanol and biodiesel | Medium-High |
| Philippines | Coconut, sugarcane | Biodiesel, ethanol | Medium |
| Vietnam | Sugarcane, cassava | Limited to growing | Medium |

## Strongest Supply Classes for Compliance Markets

1. **UCO collection and export** — established infrastructure in Indonesia, Malaysia, Thailand
2. **POME from palm oil mills** — large waste stream, underutilized, strong premium potential
3. **Animal fats** — slaughterhouse and rendering waste across the region
4. **Palm residues** — lignocellulosic potential for advanced biofuels
5. **Cassava ethanol** — Thailand leading, but conventional pathway faces caps

## Logistics and Certification
- Established shipping routes to Rotterdam (primary EU entry point)
- ISCC certification infrastructure exists in all major SE Asia producer countries
- Trading relationships with European and Asian buyers are established
- Documentary infrastructure for waste collection verification needs continued investment

## Competitive Position vs Other Supply Regions
- SE Asia waste feedstocks (UCO, POME) are competitive with European domestic supply
- SE Asia is the primary global source of palm-derived waste streams
- Brazilian sugarcane ethanol is more competitive for conventional ethanol
- US corn ethanol and soy biodiesel are dominant in the US domestic market

## Disqualifier Conditions
A SE Asia supply route should be abandoned if:
- the feedstock classification is not defensible in the destination market
- the route CI is uncompetitive after transport (mainly California)
- the destination market does not offer enough premium to justify the logistics

## Worked Example: Why SE Asia Waste Supply Is More Competitive Than SE Asia Conventional Supply
**Scenario:** A SE Asia producer can export conventional cassava ethanol or waste-based UCO HVO.

1. Conventional cassava ethanol faces commodity competition from Brazil and the US.
2. Waste-based UCO HVO unlocks premium treatment in every major market.
3. The waste route is more competitive than the conventional route despite higher production complexity.
4. SE Asia's competitive advantage lies in waste-based pathways, not conventional commodity supply.

### What this reveals
SE Asia's strongest supply position is waste-based. Conventional supply faces stiffer competition from larger, more established producers.
