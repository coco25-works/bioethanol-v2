# Wave 1 Micro-Opportunity Log

**Last updated:** 2026-04-23  
**Primary audience:** Commercial / BD team tracking short, high-value opportunity notes from Wave 1 market asymmetries  
**Decision supported:** Which subtle legal or implementation differences in Wave 1 markets create actionable opportunities

## Analytical Question
This file captures short, high-value opportunity notes created by subtle legal or implementation differences in Wave 1 markets. Each entry is a micro-opportunity that may be missed in broader market comparisons.

### How To Read This File
These are the small differences that can matter commercially. Each micro-opportunity is a case where two markets look similar, but the small difference creates a meaningful difference in value, access, or timing. The durability assessment shows how stable the opportunity is.

## Market Comparison View
This file is narrower than the ranking file. It does not assign route-level priority labels. Instead, it captures small asymmetries that can upgrade or downgrade a route once the broader market thesis is already in place.

## Regulatory Asymmetry View
The opportunities below cluster around a few repeatable asymmetry types:
- value-capture mechanics,
- waste classification boundaries,
- administrative friction,
- chain-of-custody flexibility,
- and timing windows created by new mandates or phase-ins.

## Key Findings
- The Netherlands creates an unusual concentration of gateway-style opportunities because routing flexibility and maritime adjacency reinforce each other.
- Germany creates durable opportunities where premium visibility and administrative structure are stronger than in neighboring markets.
- Several attractive notes are only real opportunities if the proof chain is unusually strong; otherwise they collapse quickly.

## Demand-Side Implications
- Micro-opportunities matter most in markets that already have real buyer pull.
- They do not rescue weak markets; they sharpen strong ones.

## Supply-Side Implications
- Feedstocks with unsettled classification, especially POME, can create upside and fragility at the same time.
- The micro-opportunity only exists if the producer can meet the associated proof burden better than competitors.

## SE Asia Producer Implications
- Use this file to improve route selection inside already-prioritized markets.
- Do not use these notes as a substitute for the main route ranking.
- The highest-value micro-opportunities are usually those where Southeast Asia has a real supply advantage and the market difference is durable enough to monetize.

## Disqualifier Conditions
- Treat any note tied to unsettled feedstock classification as non-bankable until the proof model is validated.
- Do not treat a timing-window note as durable strategy unless a second demand channel exists.
- If the opportunity depends on special documentation, registration, or trader access the team does not have, it remains a note, not an executable route.

## Worked Example or Scenario
**Scenario:** A team can place qualifying waste-based fuel into Germany or the Netherlands and notices both markets look strong in the headline ranking.

1. The broad ranking says both routes are high quality.
2. The micro-opportunity layer shows that the Netherlands may win for a specific cargo if book-and-claim handling and Rotterdam routing flexibility reduce execution friction.
3. The same file also shows that Germany may still win where THG liquidity and cleaner administrative access create better monetization.
4. The practical choice therefore depends on the cargo's proof profile and the team's route-control advantage, not just the headline market score.

### What this reveals
Micro-opportunities refine route choice inside the top tier. They do not replace the top-tier market thesis.
