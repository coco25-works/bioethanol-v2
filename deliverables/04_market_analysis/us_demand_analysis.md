# US Demand Analysis

**Last updated:** 2026-04-21
**Primary audience:** Commercial / BD team understanding US demand dynamics in depth
**Decision supported:** Why the US market looks the way it does for bio-based fuel demand

## Analytical Question
What creates US biofuel demand and how do federal and California systems differ?

### What This File Explains
The US has two very different demand systems under one geography. Federal demand is volumetric. California demand is carbon-intensity-based. They can both be attractive but for different reasons, and they create different barriers for a Southeast Asia exporter.

## Key Findings
- US federal RFS creates the largest volumetric biofuel mandate globally
- RIN credit market is the most liquid biofuel credit market globally
- California LCFS creates a parallel CI-based demand system with different pathway economics
- EPA pathway approval for foreign producers is the primary access barrier
- Domestic production (corn ethanol, soy biodiesel) is large and politically protected

## Demand Drivers
1. **RFS annual RVO**: Volume obligations on obligated parties — creates RIN demand
2. **LCFS CI benchmark**: Declining CI benchmark in California — creates credit demand
3. **D-code premiums**: D3 (cellulosic) and D4 (biomass-based diesel) RINs command premiums over D6

## Federal vs California Comparison

| Dimension | US federal (RFS) | California (LCFS) |
|---|---|---|
| Compliance design | Volumetric | CI-based |
| Transport penalty | None | Yes (CI penalty) |
| Pathway approval | EPA | CARB |
| Credit liquidity | Very high | High |
| Foreign producer barrier | High (EPA + Q-RIN) | High (CARB) |
| Waste-based premium | High (D4 RINs) | High (low CI credits) |

## Disqualifier Conditions
A US demand route should be reconsidered if:
- the company cannot obtain EPA pathway approval within a commercially acceptable timeframe
- the CI penalty in California makes the route uncompetitive after transport
- the domestic supply in the specific D-code category already saturates demand

## Worked Example: Why Federal And California Can Both Be Attractive But For Different Reasons
**Scenario:** A producer routes waste-based HVO to both US federal and California.

1. US federal rewards the route through D4 RINs based on pathway qualification.
2. California rewards the route through LCFS credits based on CI performance.
3. The same fuel behaves differently in each system because one is volumetric and one is CI-based.
4. A producer can access both, but must understand that the two systems are not interchangeable.

### What this reveals
US federal and California are two different markets under one geography. Each requires separate pathway approval and has separate economics.
