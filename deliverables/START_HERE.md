# Start Here

**Last updated:** 2026-04-23
**Purpose:** Help team members enter the bio-based fuels research and strategy pack without reading every file first.

## The Short Answer

The strongest near-term opportunity is:

**Waste-based HVO/FAME from Southeast Asia into Germany and the Netherlands.**

This works only if three conditions are true:

1. The feedstock is genuinely classifiable as waste.
2. The proof chain is strong enough for audit.
3. The contract lets the producer capture some compliance value.

If any of those fail, use the risk and no-go files before spending management time.

## What This Pack Is

This is a decision-support repository for commercial, compliance, operations, finance, and leadership teams assessing global bio-based fuel opportunities.

It is strongest for:

- market prioritization
- pathway screening
- feedstock-to-market mapping
- buyer and counterparty logic
- certification and chain-of-custody planning
- risk and no-go discipline

It is not legal advice and should not replace jurisdiction-specific legal review before contracting or investment.

## Known Caveats

- Japan remains translation-dependent except where the Fuel Quality Control Act translation is explicitly logged.
- RED III member-state implementation remains active after the May 2025 deadline.
- FuelEU Maritime has published operational implementing acts, but pathway modeling still depends on Annex I, Article 10 criteria, verifier practice, and Commission Q&A.
- Wave 2 markets are supply-context and monitoring markets, not full-depth first-wave destination analyses.
- Mandates, credit prices, and agency guidance can change. Check the watchlists before making investment or contracting decisions.
