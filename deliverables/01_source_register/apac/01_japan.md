# Japan Source Register

**Primary audience:** Commercial / BD team screening Japanese biofuel opportunities
**Decision supported:** Which Japanese regulations create demand for bio-based fuels and how the transport fuel policy works
**Coverage scope:** Japanese transport fuel policy, biofuel mandates, and official policy documents
**Baseline date:** 2026-04-23
**Wave:** 1

## Why This Market Matters

Japan operates a policy-guideline-driven biofuel framework rather than a hard mandate system comparable to the US RFS or EU RED. The primary biofuel pathway is bio-ETBE (ethyl tert-butyl ether) blending in gasoline, with a supplementary biodiesel blending pathway. Japan's 500-million-liter bioethanol-equivalent target (expressed in various METI policy documents including the Green Growth Strategy) creates directional demand, but the regulatory mechanism is softer: government sets targets, industry associations coordinate voluntary compliance, and METI monitors through reporting rather than penalty-based quota surrender. This structure changes commercial risk assessment — demand signals are real but enforcement friction is lower, and value capture depends on voluntary industry alignment rather than statutory credit trading.

### What this means for non-specialists
Japan matters strategically but is weaker commercially because there is no tradable credit system. The evidence layer is also weaker: primary sources are in Japanese, and translation gaps are flagged as high-priority issues.
