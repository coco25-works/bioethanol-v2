## Why This Market Matters
Singapore is the world's largest bunkering port and a major regional trading hub. While Singapore does not have a biofuel mandate comparable to EU or US systems, its role in maritime fuel supply makes it relevant for biofuel bunkering under the context of FuelEU Maritime and IMO GHG reduction targets. The Maritime and Port Authority (MPA) has been piloting biofuel bunkering. Singapore also serves as a pricing benchmark and trading hub for SE Asia biofuel exports.

### What this means for non-specialists
Singapore is a logistics hub, not a compliance market. The sources below show that Singapore's relevance is operational and maritime-linked, not mandate-driven.
