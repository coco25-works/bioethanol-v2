## Why This Market Matters

California's Low Carbon Fuel Standard (LCFS) operates as a carbon-intensity compliance system that creates demand for low-CI fuels through tradable credits and deficits. Unlike federal RFS, which is volumetric, LCFS is CI-based — fuels generating credits below a declining benchmark and fuels above generating deficits. The pathway CI scoring, registry and reporting mechanics, credit trading, and value-capture logic create specific commercial asymmetries versus federal RFS, EU member-state systems, UK RTFO, and other frameworks. CARB pathway approval determines the CI value for each fuel pathway, making it the gatekeeper for credit generation. The LCFS credit market is one of the most liquid sub-national compliance credit markets globally.

### What this means for non-specialists
California rewards low-carbon routes, but it penalizes long-distance supply because transport adds to the CI score. The sources below prove that the LCFS framework and CARB pathway-approval process are real and legally grounded. All sources are in English.

## Key Asymmetries vs Federal RFS

| Dimension | California LCFS | Federal RFS |
|---|---|---|
| Metric basis | Carbon intensity (CI) based — credits/deficits generated by CI differential from benchmark | Volumetric basis — RIN generated per gallon of qualifying fuel |
| Compliance mechanism | Obligated parties must maintain net-zero or positive credit balance | Obligated parties must acquire and retire RINs to meet volumetric obligations |
| Credit ownership | Credits owned by fuel pathway holders; tradable in LCFS Credit Bank and Transfer Registry | RINs generated by producers/importers; tradable D-RIN market |
| Pathway gatekeeping | CARB pathway approval determines CI value — direct gatekeeper for credit generation | EPA registration and pathway approval; but RIN value less CI-dependent |
| Lifecycle model | CA-GREET (CARB-specific) with feedstock and process granularity | EPA-modified GREET for some pathways; default values for others |
| Benchmark trajectory | Declining CI benchmark through 2045 (2024 amendments) | Volumetric mandates set by statute with EPA annual rulemaking |
| Market structure | Sub-national (California); CARB-administered credit registry | Federal (national); EPA-administered RIN system |
| Credit banking | Allowed but limited by 2024 amendments (banking limits tightened) | RIN banking allowed; RINs have expiration |
