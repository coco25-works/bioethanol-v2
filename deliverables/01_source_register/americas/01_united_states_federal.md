**Baseline date:** 2026-04-21

## Why This Market Matters
The US federal Renewable Fuel Standard is the single largest demand-side mandate for biofuels globally by volume. Established under CAA Section 211(o) and expanded by the Energy Independence and Security Act of 2007, the RFS2 program requires obligated parties (refiners and importers of gasoline and diesel) to blend specified volumes of renewable fuels or acquire RIN credits. The RIN system creates a liquid tradable credit market with distinct D-code categories. Unlike EU member-state systems where credit ownership mechanics and implementing agencies vary, US federal compliance is centralized through EPA and the EMTS platform. The RFS program drives billions of gallons of annual biofuel demand and generates a multi-billion-dollar RIN credit market.

### What this means for non-specialists
The US federal market is large and valuable, but the main barrier for foreign producers is the regulatory access process (EPA pathway approval). The sources below prove that the RFS, RIN system, and EPA administration are all backed by US federal law. All sources are in English.
