## Why This Market Matters
The Netherlands operates one of the most consequential EU member-state implementations of the Renewable Energy Directive through its HBE (Hernieuwbare Brandstoffen Eenheden — Renewable Fuel Units) system. The framework, embedded in the Wet milieubeheer and implemented through the Besluit energie vervoer and Regeling energie vervoer, creates direct compliance demand for low-carbon fuels and generates tradable credits. The Dutch HBE system is unique in its three-tier premium differentiation: HBE-A (advanced biofuels from Annex IX Part A feedstocks), HBE-O (other biofuels), and HBE-G (green gas/RNG), each commanding different market values. As Europe's largest bunkering and trading hub (Rotterdam), the Netherlands is a pivotal market for biofuel value capture.

### What this means for non-specialists
The Netherlands matters for two reasons: it is a compliance market with premium treatment for the right pathways, and it is the main European entry port for Southeast Asia seaborne supply. The sources below prove both roles are backed by Dutch law.
