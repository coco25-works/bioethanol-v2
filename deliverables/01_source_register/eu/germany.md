## Why This Market Matters
Germany operates one of the most consequential EU member-state implementations of the Renewable Energy Directive through its transport-fuel GHG reduction quota system (`THG-Quote`). The core demand pull for transport biofuels sits in the Federal Immission Control framework and its implementing ordinances, not in Germany's separate national fuel-emissions trading law. In practice, Germany creates direct compliance demand for low-carbon fuels and one of the most liquid biofuel credit markets in Europe. Germany's specific quota accounting rules, proof burden requirements, and value-capture mechanics differ from other EU markets in ways that can change commercial access and premium capture.

### What this means for non-specialists
Germany is one of the two most important European markets in this repository. The sources below prove the demand mechanism, the proof requirements, and the credit-market structure are all backed by real German law. Non-German speakers should note that the key laws are in German; translation gaps are flagged but the content is well-documented internationally.
