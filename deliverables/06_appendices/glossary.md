# Glossary

**Last updated:** 2026-04-21  
**Purpose:** Define key terms used across this repository so the reader never has to guess.

## How To Use This File
- Terms are listed alphabetically for quick lookup.
- Cross-references show where the term is especially important in the main files.
- Terms are defined in the context of biofuel regulation and market access, not in a generic sense.

### Why This File Matters
This repository uses specialized terms from biofuel regulation, compliance markets, and supply-chain proof systems. If a term is unfamiliar, find it here first.

## Terms

**Advanced biofuel** — A biofuel produced from feedstocks defined in Annex IX Part A of RED II/III (waste, residues, lignocellulosic material). Receives premium regulatory treatment (double counting in EU, D3 RINs in US). See [03_biofuel_reference/lignocellulosic_feedstocks.md](../03_biofuel_reference/lignocellulosic_feedstocks.md).

**Annex IX** — The annex to RED II/III that categorizes feedstocks for biofuel counting. Part A feedstocks receive double counting. Part B feedstocks do not. Critical for pathway economics. See [01_source_register/eu/eu_level.md](../01_source_register/eu/eu_level.md).

**BAFA** — Bundesamt fur Wirtschaft und Ausfuhrkontrolle. German federal agency associated with administration and compliance handling in the transport-fuel GHG quota system. See [02_country_reference/eu/germany.md](../02_country_reference/eu/germany.md).

**BEHG** — Brennstoffemissionshandelsgesetz. Germany's national fuel-emissions trading law. It is relevant background context but is not the main legal engine of the transport-biofuel market described in this repository. See [01_source_register/eu/germany.md](../01_source_register/eu/germany.md).

**Bio-ETBE** — Ethyl tert-butyl ether produced from bioethanol. Japan's preferred biofuel blending pathway (rather than direct ethanol blending). See [02_country_reference/apac/japan.md](../02_country_reference/apac/japan.md).

**Biomass-based diesel** — US RFS category (D4) encompassing biodiesel (FAME) and renewable diesel (HVO). See [02_country_reference/americas/united_states_federal.md](../02_country_reference/americas/united_states_federal.md).

**Book-and-claim** — A chain-of-custody model where sustainability attributes are tracked separately from physical fuel flow. Accepted in Netherlands and UK; more restricted in Germany and France. See [03_biofuel_reference/certifications_and_chain_of_custody.md](../03_biofuel_reference/certifications_and_chain_of_custody.md).

**CARB** — California Air Resources Board. Implements the LCFS. See [02_country_reference/americas/california.md](../02_country_reference/americas/california.md).

**Chain of custody** — The system for tracking sustainability attributes of biofuel from feedstock origin through production to end use. Models include mass balance, book-and-claim, and identity preserved. See [03_biofuel_reference/certifications_and_chain_of_custody.md](../03_biofuel_reference/certifications_and_chain_of_custody.md).

**CI (Carbon Intensity)** — Lifecycle greenhouse gas emissions per unit of energy, measured in gCO2e/MJ. Used in California LCFS and EU GHG methodology. See [02_country_reference/americas/california.md](../02_country_reference/americas/california.md).

**Conventional biofuel** — Biofuel produced from food or feed crops (rapeseed, soybean, palm, corn, sugarcane, etc.). Capped under RED (7%) and faces lower premiums than advanced biofuels. See [03_biofuel_reference/bioethanol.md](../03_biofuel_reference/bioethanol.md).

**D-code** — US RFS classification for different renewable fuel types. D3 (cellulosic), D4 (biomass-based diesel), D5 (advanced), D6 (renewable fuel), D7 (cellulosic diesel). Each generates different RIN types with different values. See [02_country_reference/americas/united_states_federal.md](../02_country_reference/americas/united_states_federal.md).

**Double counting** — EU regulatory treatment where Annex IX Part A feedstocks count twice toward the transport renewable energy target. Creates significant per-liter value premium. See [01_source_register/eu/eu_level.md](../01_source_register/eu/eu_level.md).

**EPA** — US Environmental Protection Agency. Implements the RFS program. See [02_country_reference/americas/united_states_federal.md](../02_country_reference/americas/united_states_federal.md).

**EMTS** — EPA Moderated Transaction System. The registry and trading platform for RIN credits. See [01_source_register/americas/united_states_federal.md](../01_source_register/americas/united_states_federal.md).

**FAME** — Fatty acid methyl ester. The technical name for biodiesel produced through transesterification of lipid feedstocks. See [03_biofuel_reference/biodiesel_and_fame.md](../03_biofuel_reference/biodiesel_and_fame.md).

**FuelEU Maritime** — EU regulation creating a GHG intensity obligation for ships at EU ports from 2025. See [01_source_register/eu/eu_level.md](../01_source_register/eu/eu_level.md).

**GHG intensity** — Greenhouse gas intensity. Lifecycle CO2-equivalent emissions per megajoule of fuel energy. Used in EU, California, and FuelEU Maritime compliance. See [02_country_reference/americas/california.md](../02_country_reference/americas/california.md).

**HBE** — Hernieuwbare Brandstoffen Eenheden (Renewable Fuel Units). The Netherlands' compliance system with three tiers: HBE-A (waste-based), HBE-O (conventional), HBE-G (green gas). See [02_country_reference/eu/netherlands.md](../02_country_reference/eu/netherlands.md).

**HEFA** — Hydroprocessed esters and fatty acids. A SAF conversion route using lipid feedstocks. See [03_biofuel_reference/saf_linked_pathways.md](../03_biofuel_reference/saf_linked_pathways.md).

**HVO** — Hydrotreated vegetable oil. Also called renewable diesel. A drop-in fuel produced by hydrotreatment of lipid feedstocks. See [03_biofuel_reference/renewable_diesel_hvo.md](../03_biofuel_reference/renewable_diesel_hvo.md).

**ILUC** — Indirect land-use change. The concept that biofuel production from certain feedstocks may cause deforestation or land conversion elsewhere. Under EU RED III, high-ILUC feedstocks (palm oil) face phase-out by 2030. See [01_source_register/eu/eu_level.md](../01_source_register/eu/eu_level.md).

**ISCC** — International Sustainability and Carbon Certification. The most widely used sustainability certification scheme for biofuels in the EU. See [03_biofuel_reference/certifications_and_chain_of_custody.md](../03_biofuel_reference/certifications_and_chain_of_custody.md).

**LCFS** — Low Carbon Fuel Standard. California's carbon-intensity-based biofuel compliance system. See [02_country_reference/americas/california.md](../02_country_reference/americas/california.md).

**Mass balance** — A chain-of-custody model where sustainability attributes are tracked proportionally through a production or supply chain. The primary model accepted under EU rules. See [03_biofuel_reference/certifications_and_chain_of_custody.md](../03_biofuel_reference/certifications_and_chain_of_custody.md).

**Obligated party** — A fuel supplier or importer required to comply with a biofuel mandate. The party who must blend biofuels or purchase credits. See country reference files for each jurisdiction.

**POME** — Palm oil mill effluent. A waste stream from palm oil production. When classified as waste, POME-derived biofuel receives premium treatment (Annex IX Part A double counting). See [03_biofuel_reference/waste_oils_and_residues.md](../03_biofuel_reference/waste_oils_and_residues.md).

**Q-RIN** — Quality-assured RIN. A RIN generated by a foreign facility that has undergone third-party engineering review. Required for foreign biofuel producers to generate RINs for the US market. See [02_country_reference/americas/united_states_federal.md](../02_country_reference/americas/united_states_federal.md).

**RED II** — Renewable Energy Directive (EU) 2018/2001. The core EU directive governing biofuel demand, sustainability criteria, and counting rules. See [01_source_register/eu/eu_level.md](../01_source_register/eu/eu_level.md).

**RED III** — Directive (EU) 2023/2413 amending RED II. Raises transport targets and tightens feedstock caps. Transposition deadline May 2025. See [01_source_register/eu/eu_level.md](../01_source_register/eu/eu_level.md).

**ReFuelEU Aviation** — EU regulation mandating SAF blending at EU airports from 2025 (2% → 70% by 2050). See [01_source_register/eu/eu_level.md](../01_source_register/eu/eu_level.md).

**RFNBO** — Renewable fuel of non-biological origin. Synthetic fuels (e-methanol, e-kerosene) produced from renewable hydrogen and captured CO2. Receive potential multiplier treatment under FuelEU Maritime. See [03_biofuel_reference/biomethanol.md](../03_biofuel_reference/biomethanol.md).

**RFS** — Renewable Fuel Standard. The US federal biofuel mandate under Clean Air Act §211(o). See [02_country_reference/americas/united_states_federal.md](../02_country_reference/americas/united_states_federal.md).

**RIN** — Renewable Identification Number. The tradable compliance credit under the US RFS program. See [02_country_reference/americas/united_states_federal.md](../02_country_reference/americas/united_states_federal.md).

**RNG** — Renewable natural gas. Upgraded biogas suitable for pipeline injection or vehicle use. See [03_biofuel_reference/biogas_and_rng.md](../03_biofuel_reference/biogas_and_rng.md).

**RSB** — Roundtable on Sustainable Biomaterials. An alternative sustainability certification scheme with growing acceptance. See [03_biofuel_reference/certifications_and_chain_of_custody.md](../03_biofuel_reference/certifications_and_chain_of_custody.md).

**RTFO** — Renewable Transport Fuel Obligation. The UK's biofuel mandate. See [02_country_reference/eu/uk.md](../02_country_reference/eu/uk.md).

**RTFC** — Renewable Transport Fuel Certificate. The tradable compliance credit under the UK RTFO. See [02_country_reference/eu/uk.md](../02_country_reference/eu/uk.md).

**SAF** — Sustainable aviation fuel. Aviation fuel produced from renewable feedstocks through approved conversion pathways. See [03_biofuel_reference/saf_linked_pathways.md](../03_biofuel_reference/saf_linked_pathways.md).

**THG-Quota** — Treibhausgasminderungsquote (GHG reduction quota). Germany's compliance system under BEHG. Creates tradable THG credits. See [02_country_reference/eu/germany.md](../02_country_reference/eu/germany.md).

**TIRUERT** — Taux d'incorporation des biocarburants. France's volumetric biofuel blending obligation. See [02_country_reference/eu/france.md](../02_country_reference/eu/france.md).

**UCO** — Used cooking oil. The most demanded waste feedstock for biofuel globally. Receives Annex IX Part A double counting in the EU. See [03_biofuel_reference/waste_oils_and_residues.md](../03_biofuel_reference/waste_oils_and_residues.md).
