# Team Share Readiness Review

**Last updated:** 2026-04-23
**Scope:** All markdown documents under `deliverables/`, with emphasis on whether the repository is accurate enough, explainable enough, and comprehensive enough to share with a mixed commercial, compliance, operations, finance, and leadership team.

## Overall Verdict

The deliverables are ready to share as a decision-support pack, with clear caveats.

The strongest use case is internal strategy alignment: market prioritization, buyer logic, pathway screening, certification requirements, and no-go discipline. The repository should not be treated as legal advice or as a fully current statutory database for every jurisdiction.

## Residual Caveats

These should be communicated when sharing the pack:

- Japanese sources remain translation-dependent except where explicitly noted.
- RED III member-state implementation remains active after the May 2025 transposition deadline.
- FuelEU Maritime has published operational implementing acts, but pathway modeling still depends on Annex I, Article 10 criteria, verifier practice, and Commission Q&A.
- Wave 2 markets are useful for supply context and monitoring, but not built to the same depth as Wave 1 destination markets.
- Credit prices, mandate percentages, and agency guidance can change; use the watchlists before making investment or contracting decisions.

## Bottom Line For The Team

The main conclusion is explainable and well-supported:

**Waste-based HVO/FAME routes from Southeast Asia to Germany and the Netherlands remain the strongest near-term opportunities.**

The conclusion depends on three operating assumptions:

1. Feedstock is genuinely waste-classifiable.
2. Documentation and certification survive audit.
3. Contracts preserve some compliance value for the producer.

If any of those assumptions fail, the strategy should be paused or rerouted through the no-go conditions and risk files.
