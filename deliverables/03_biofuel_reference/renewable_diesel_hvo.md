# Renewable Diesel / HVO — Cross-Market Pathway Reference

**Last updated:** 2026-04-21  
**Primary audience:** Commercial / BD, compliance, operations, finance, and strategy teams  
**Decision supported:** Where HVO demand is strongest, why it often outranks FAME commercially, and how Southeast Asia supply fits the route

## What This File Helps You Decide
Use this file when the team needs to answer:

**When is HVO the best liquid-fuel route for Southeast Asia supply, and why does it often outperform FAME?**

The short answer is that HVO is often the strongest premium export pathway for waste-based lipid feedstocks because it combines:
- strong regulatory treatment,
- better downstream usability,
- and broad buyer acceptance.

## Executive Takeaway
If a Southeast Asia producer can reliably produce **waste-based HVO**, it usually has one of the strongest export routes in the entire repository.

Why:
- waste-based HVO receives premium treatment in the main target markets,
- buyers often prefer it to FAME because it behaves more like a drop-in fuel,
- and it can fit both road-fuel and some maritime-linked opportunity channels.

The route is not automatically easy, though.

It depends on:
- defensible waste classification,
- certification strength,
- and a production base that can support the higher-capital route.

## Why This Pathway Matters
HVO matters because it often combines the best features of the most attractive liquid-fuel export routes:
- premium treatment for the right feedstocks,
- fewer blending and operability constraints than FAME,
- and strong buyer familiarity in the main target markets.

For a mixed team, the easiest way to think about HVO is this:

**If FAME is the established workhorse, HVO is often the premium-grade version for the same broad feedstock family.**

## How To Read This File
This file is not asking whether HVO is technically good fuel. It is asking whether HVO is the **best monetizable route** for a given feedstock and destination.

The answer usually depends on:
1. whether the feedstock qualifies as waste,
2. whether the market rewards waste-based routes clearly,
3. whether the producer can justify the higher-capital conversion route,
4. and whether HVO's operational advantages over FAME matter to the buyer.

## Feedstock and Conversion-Route Scope

### In scope
- HVO from `UCO`
- HVO from animal fats / tallow
- HVO from conventional vegetable oils
- HVO from selected waste-based palm-side streams where classification is defensible
- refinery-based and hydrotreatment configurations

### Out of scope
- FAME
- Fischer-Tropsch diesel
- SAF-specific routes except where they overlap commercially with HVO logic

### Key distinction
HVO and FAME can come from similar feedstocks, but they do not behave the same commercially.

HVO often wins because it is easier for downstream fuel systems to use and easier for buyers to integrate operationally.

## What HVO Really Is In Commercial Terms
For non-specialists, the practical value of HVO is that it is usually easier to sell into serious fuel systems because it behaves more like a conventional drop-in diesel product.

That matters because the buyer is often not just paying for the compliance value. The buyer is also paying for easier operational use.

## Regulatory Demand by Market
This table shows where HVO is strongest as a commercial route.

| Market | Demand mechanism | HVO treatment | Practical value level |
|---|---|---|---|
| Germany | THG-Quote | Very strong for waste-based HVO | Very High |
| Netherlands | HBE system | Very strong for premium waste-based HVO | Very High |
| France | TIRUERT | Viable for strong non-palm routes, weaker for palm-related routes | High |
| US federal | RFS | Strong if route qualifies for valuable category treatment | High |
| California | LCFS | Strong low-CI route, but distance-sensitive | High |
| UK | RTFO | Strong for waste-based and development-fuel-relevant routes | High |
| Japan | Policy-guided market | Limited direct near-term HVO pull | Low |

### What this table is really saying
HVO is one of the few pathways that remains attractive across almost every serious market in the repository.

The main difference is not whether markets like HVO. They usually do. The difference is how much they pay for the right version of HVO.

## Eligibility and Restrictions by Market
- Waste-based HVO is usually the strongest commercial version of the route.
- Conventional HVO is weaker where conventional biofuel caps or feedstock restrictions bite.
- Palm-related HVO routes face the same broader pressure that affects palm-based liquid biofuels in Europe.
- Co-processing treatment can be more complex and less commercially clean than straight renewable-route HVO.

## Certification and Traceability Requirements
HVO depends on the same core proof logic as other premium waste-based pathways:
- sustainability certification,
- feedstock origin proof,
- chain-of-custody integrity,
- and route-specific regulatory approval where relevant.

### What this means in practice
The pathway may be operationally easier for the buyer than FAME, but the documentation burden is not lighter. If anything, the higher-value route often deserves even more disciplined proof control.

## Route Competitiveness and Proof Burden

### Why HVO often beats FAME
- it behaves more like conventional diesel,
- it avoids some downstream blending and operability issues,
- and it is often easier for buyers to integrate at scale.

### Why HVO does not automatically win
- the route is more capital-intensive,
- it depends heavily on strong waste-feedstock economics,
- and it still fails commercially if the proof chain is weak.

### Best-fit HVO routes
- `UCO -> HVO`
- `animal fats -> HVO`
- carefully defended waste-based palm-side routes, where accepted

## Cross-Market Asymmetry Map

| Asymmetry | Markets compared | Why it matters commercially |
|---|---|---|
| Waste premium visibility | Germany vs Netherlands | Both strong, but the value mechanics show up differently |
| Distance sensitivity | California vs Europe | California can weaken long-distance HVO economics |

| Palm-route treatment | France / wider EU vs other markets | Palm-related HVO routes face much weaker strategic footing in Europe |
| Co-processing treatment | Market to market | Commercial value can change significantly based on classification and accounting treatment |

## Southeast Asia Supply Implications
HVO is one of the strongest strategic fits for Southeast Asia where the producer has access to credible waste feedstocks.

### Best fit
- `UCO -> HVO`
- `POME -> HVO` where classification and buyer acceptance are strong
- animal-fat-based HVO where available

### More sensitive fit
- routes involving waste streams that require stronger classification defense

### Weak fit
- conventional palm-based HVO into Europe as a flagship thesis

## Worked Example: Why HVO Often Becomes The Lead Liquid Route
**Scenario:** A producer can choose between exporting waste-based FAME or waste-based HVO.

1. Both may qualify for premium treatment if the feedstock is strong.
2. HVO is often easier for the buyer to integrate as a diesel substitute.
3. That ease of use can improve buyer interest and route durability.
4. If the producer has the right asset base, HVO often becomes the lead commercial route.

### What this reveals
The value of HVO is not only in the regulation. It is in the combination of regulation **and operational usability**.

## What Non-Experts Usually Miss
- HVO is not just “another biodiesel.” It is often commercially superior because of downstream usability.
- The strongest HVO route is usually not about conventional oils. It is about waste-based feedstocks with strong proof.
- HVO can still be strategically weak if the route depends on a politically exposed feedstock.

## What This Means For Our Team
- If the company can produce waste-based HVO reliably, it should treat that route as one of the strongest export plays in the repository.
- Europe-first still makes the most sense for the best waste-based HVO routes.
- The US is valuable, but second-wave because access is heavier.
- California is worth monitoring where the route is exceptionally strong on CI, but not as a first destination for long-distance supply.
