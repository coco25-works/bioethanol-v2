# Waste Oils and Residues - Cross-Market Feedstock Reference

**Last updated:** 2026-04-23  
**Primary audience:** Commercial / BD team assessing waste feedstock treatment across markets  
**Decision supported:** Why waste classes attract premiums, where fraud concerns create limits, and how these issues map onto major destination markets

## What This File Helps You Decide
- whether a specific waste stream qualifies for premium treatment in the target market
- how much documentary and certification effort is required to unlock that premium
- which waste classes carry the highest fraud or reclassification risk
- whether feedstock uncertainty makes a route unsuitable for premium positioning

## Executive Takeaway
Waste oils and residues are the highest-value biofuel feedstock class, but only if waste status can be proved.

Every major market rewards credible waste classification. The commercial upside is real. The proof burden is also real. UCO remains the cleanest waste-based route, but fraud risk is high. PFAD remains disputed. POME can be commercially interesting, but its premium treatment is conditional on jurisdiction-specific acceptance and strong mill-level proof. The practical lesson is simple: waste premiums are real, but they are not automatic.

## Why This Pathway Matters
Waste classification is one of the strongest value drivers in the repository because it affects route eligibility, premium treatment, and buyer willingness to pay across Europe, the US, and the UK.

## Feedstock and Conversion-Route Scope
- UCO and animal fats are the most versatile feedstocks for liquid fuel routes
- POME links both to HVO and biogas pathways
- PFAD route eligibility is less controversial than PFAD waste-premium claims

1. **UCO is broadly accepted, but verification intensity differs.**
2. **POME is a conditional opportunity, not a settled baseline.**
3. **PFAD is commercially usable, but risky to model as a waste-premium route.**
4. **Animal fats and tallow are more settled and often easier to defend than UCO.**

## Regulatory Demand by Market

### EU
Waste-derived routes can capture unusually strong value because qualifying feedstocks receive favorable treatment under the EU framework.

### US
Waste routes remain valuable, but the proof and pathway-approval burden is more operationally demanding.

### UK
The UK remains commercially relevant because waste-based and development-fuel logic can still reward the right routes.

### California
California values low-CI outcomes rather than waste labels alone, but waste-derived routes often benefit indirectly.

## Certification and Traceability Requirements
- collection-point records matter
- mass-balance discipline matters
- certification is necessary but not enough on its own
- POME specifically requires mill-level documentation strong enough to survive challenge

## Southeast Asia Supply Implications

### UCO
- strongest near-term waste-based route
- large enough supply base to matter
- fraud risk means verification quality directly affects price

### POME
- very large supply base
- premium potential exists only when residue treatment is accepted and proven
- most mills do not yet maintain proof chains strong enough for a bankable default route
- should be treated as a `Selective` opportunity, not a universal waste-premium assumption

### PFAD
- significant volume, but classification remains disputed
- should not be modeled as a settled waste-premium route

### Animal fats and tallow
- commercially strong where traceability is good
- often easier to defend than UCO

## Worked Example: UCO Versus POME Into Europe
**Scenario:** A producer can build either a UCO-based HVO route or a POME-based HVO route into Europe.

1. Both feedstocks can look attractive on paper.
2. UCO is easier to defend because market acceptance is broader and audit expectations are better understood.
3. POME can still outperform in upside terms if the proof chain is unusually strong and the counterparty accepts the residue case.
4. If that proof chain is weak, the POME route can lose the premium while the UCO route remains bankable.

### What this reveals
POME is not automatically a bad route. It is a conditional route that should sit behind stronger settled waste pathways unless the documentation and buyer case are already in place.

## Fraud Concerns and Verification Tightening
- UCO fraud remains a known issue
- waste premiums attract scrutiny precisely because the uplift is real
- documentation failure usually destroys value faster than technical fuel failure

## What Non-Experts Usually Miss
- waste classification is not binary across markets
- premium value and compliance cost rise together
- POME is not automatically waste for premium purposes
- PFAD uncertainty is genuine, not cosmetic

## What This Means for Our Team
- prioritize UCO and other settled waste streams first
- treat POME as a selective high-upside route that requires early buyer and certification validation
- do not build a premium case around PFAD unless market-specific legal treatment is clear
- budget for documentation systems as commercial infrastructure, not administrative overhead
