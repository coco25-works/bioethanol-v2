# Biogas and RNG Strategy

**Last updated:** 2026-04-21  
**Primary audience:** Commercial / BD, strategy, and operations teams  
**Decision supported:** Whether biogas / RNG should be a core export route or a selective long-term route

## Executive Takeaway
Treat biogas / RNG as **strategically interesting but operationally constrained**.

It can create high value in the right market, but it is harder to move and monetize than the core liquid-fuel routes.

## Recommendation
- **Monitor** for export routes, with build-first relevance for future capability development
- stronger relevance for domestic, regional, or future maritime-linked use than for immediate flagship export strategy

## Why Now
- premium treatment exists in some markets
- waste-gas routes can become strategically stronger if maritime and transport infrastructure evolves

## What Must Be True First
- workable upgrading and delivery infrastructure
- transport-linked end use
- feedstock proof and route integrity

## Better Alternative
For near-term commercial execution, prioritize waste-based HVO / biodiesel. Keep gas routes on the strategic build list.

## What Would Make Us Stop
- infrastructure remains too weak
- economics depend on unrealistic logistics assumptions
- buyer pull does not materialize outside niche segments
