# Biodiesel and HVO Strategy

**Last updated:** 2026-04-21  
**Primary audience:** Commercial / BD, compliance, operations, finance, and leadership teams  
**Decision supported:** How the company should think about biodiesel and HVO as export pathways

## What This File Helps You Decide
Use this file when the team needs to answer:

**Which liquid-fuel route should we build around first: biodiesel / FAME, HVO, or both?**

## Executive Takeaway
Build the strategy around **waste-based HVO first**, with waste-based FAME as a supporting route where the economics and asset base justify it.

### Bottom line
- `HVO` is the strongest premium route
- `FAME` is still commercially useful, especially for UCO-based routes
- conventional palm-based liquid routes into Europe should not anchor the strategy

## Why This Matters Now
- Europe is rewarding premium waste-based liquid routes now
- Germany and the Netherlands already create real buyer pull now
- the strongest waste-based routes already fit the region's feedstock position

## Who Actually Buys
- obligated fuel suppliers
- traders and aggregators
- importers with compliance-market access
- Rotterdam-linked counterparties

## Recommended Strategic Posture
### Priority 1
- waste-based HVO into Germany and the Netherlands

### Priority 2
- waste-based FAME into Germany and the Netherlands where HVO is not the preferred route
- UK as a second-wave supporting market

### Selective
- US federal if the company is willing to fund access and approval burden

### Avoid as flagship routes
- conventional palm-based liquid-fuel routes into Europe

## What Must Be True First
- strong waste classification and documentation
- recognized certification
- buyer access into Germany / Netherlands
- ability to protect producer value capture in contract structure

## Better Alternative Logic
If the company cannot support HVO technically or financially, then strong waste-based FAME can still be a serious route. But if both are available, HVO should usually lead.

## What Would Make Us Stop
- key waste classification no longer defensible
- producer cannot protect enough value capture
- certification or proof discipline fails
- premium route economics are overtaken by weaker but easier alternatives
