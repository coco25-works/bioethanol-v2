# Bioethanol Strategy

**Last updated:** 2026-04-21  
**Primary audience:** Commercial / BD, strategy, and leadership teams  
**Decision supported:** Whether bioethanol should be a primary, selective, or secondary strategic route

## Executive Takeaway
Do **not** build the main growth story around conventional bioethanol.

Treat:
- conventional ethanol as an **Avoid** route for primary growth strategy,
- advanced ethanol as a **Selective** build-first opportunity.

## Recommendation
- **Selective:** advanced / lignocellulosic ethanol
- **Avoid:** conventional ethanol as a flagship export route

## Why Now
Advanced-route interest exists because premium treatment is available in some markets, but the route still needs technology and project readiness.

## Who Actually Buys
- buyers looking for advanced-route compliance value
- counterparties in markets that still reward advanced ethanol clearly

## What Must Be True First
- credible advanced conversion route
- real feedstock basis beyond commodity conventional supply
- proof and certification strong enough to support premium treatment

## Better Alternative
If the company has strong waste-oil routes, prioritize those ahead of ethanol. Ethanol should only move up if the route clearly escapes commodity competition.

## What Would Make Us Stop
- no credible advanced route
- no real differentiation from incumbent ethanol suppliers
- project economics depend on commodity-market assumptions rather than premium logic
