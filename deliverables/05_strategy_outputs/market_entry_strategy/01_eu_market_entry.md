# EU Market Entry Strategy

**Last updated:** 2026-04-21  
**Primary audience:** Commercial / BD, compliance, operations, and leadership teams  
**Decision supported:** How the company should enter Europe-first and what sequence makes sense

## Executive Takeaway
Enter Europe through **Germany and the Netherlands first**.

Germany gives stronger visible compliance value. The Netherlands adds gateway and routing strength. Together they form the strongest first-wave European entry logic.

## Recommended Entry Sequence
1. Secure certification and waste documentation
2. Build Rotterdam-linked logistics and buyer access
3. Target Germany and Netherlands in parallel
4. Add UK as secondary route if needed

## Why This Sequence Works
- strongest current monetization
- strongest fit for waste-based Southeast Asia supply
- best mix of buyer pull and practical import access

## What Must Be True First
- recognized certification
- audit-ready waste proof
- buyer and importer access
- value-capture discipline in contracting

## What Would Make Us Stop
- inability to defend feedstock classification
- inability to preserve meaningful producer value capture
